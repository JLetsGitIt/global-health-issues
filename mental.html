Mental Health

What is mental health?
Mental health refers to cognitive, behavioral, and emotional well-being. It is all about how people think, feel, and behave. Mental health can affect daily living, relationships, and physical health, as conditions such as stress, depression, and anxiety can be very disruptive. Continuous social and economic pressure as well as biological factors are risk factors for mental health conditions.


Statistics
Due to the recent COVID-19 and political unrest and uncertainty, many across the world are experiencing trouble maintaining their mental health. Social isolation and being overworked has become an increasing problem with 53% of adults reporting that their mental health has been negatively impacted due to worry and stress over the coronavirus. A recent review of health care professionals found a 23% prevalence of depression and anxiety, and 39% prevalence of insomnia during COVID-19.


How can I take care of my mental health?
There are many ways in which you can take care of your emotional wellbeing! Below are just a few:

  • Exercise
  • Stay connected with others
  • Set goals and priorities
  • Practice gratitude and meditation
  • Eat healthy and get plenty of sleep
